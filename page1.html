1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

For the past few decades the world has continued to progress forward when it comes to technology. We have gone from the first mobile phone being bigger than a brick to having 7 inch screens thinner than a human finger. Ultimately we have a mini - computer in our pockets as it can do almost anything we want it to from planning work or calling people. In today’s day and age nearly every business industry relies on computers and machines to help reduce business costs and increase production efficiency. For example, on some motorways in the United Kingdom they have Starbucks drive-through at the service stations. Usually at these drive-throughs they have Starbucks employees working there, but at some of them they have a machine that takes the order, then the payment and then has a tray system that sticks out in order to hand the customer their drink. This is an example of where IT systems and coding has replaced a human that had a certain amount of functions and instructions. Another example is, in libraries or law/accounting firms instead of storing large amounts of files and folders (hard copies) that would take up physical space they have a bookkeeping software installed on computers or other electronic devices that may be networked together. There are several reasons that business are introducing IT into themselves. For example, to save money by replacing employees with technology that has been programmed to do a human’s job. Also, by using technology to complete certain jobs it may reduce the chance of errors being made for example the wrong food order being given out at a food place.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

When creating digital projects people often work in teams and they need a way to communicate with each other and share work where everyone stays up to date. This is where software such as Github and Slack come into use. Slack is a software that is made for group conversations/channels and it allows you to share images, message individuals/team and share code. Github is a very similar service but is more dedicated to a team of people working on the same code from different devices. One team member can upload code to their Github user area and when another team member wants to edit or update something they would be able to clone the code into their own document and then paste it back into Github as an updated version of code. The rest of the team members would also be notified as to what changes have been made and by who. Some digital projects may require coding and for that some may use the Visual Studio Code software. This is a software that runs extensions such as Python in order for people to programme things such as text based games, creating websites and developing Artificial Intelligence. Teams may also need something similar to a notice board so each member can keep track of where the project is up to and what else needs doing. Trello is a service that covers this aspect as it allows users to create a task board that everyone can access from different devices. The user is able to create titles for their task columns such as ‘To Do’, ‘Doing’ and ‘Done’. This helps keep the project organised and on track.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The General Data Protection Regulation protects individuals as it is a way to ensure their information is protected. Websites must comply to GDPR laws especially when they are dealing with sensitive customer information such as card details, house address and email/passwords. The GDPR law was introduced in April 2016 in order to replace the 1995 Data Protection Directive [2]. The main aim of this law is to ensure that data all over Europe is protected properly. The websites have to comply by this law because they process most of the words data including information that if in the wrong hands someone’s identity can be stolen and fraudulent actions can be committed.
